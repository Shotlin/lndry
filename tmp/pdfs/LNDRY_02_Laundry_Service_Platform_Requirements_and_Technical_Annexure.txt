Document status and hierarchy
This document is designed to be attached to the project agreement as the technical and functional annexure. It 
consolidates the approved workflow into implementable requirements. Where this annexure conflicts with an informal 
message or verbal statement, the signed agreement and signed annexure control.
Scope lock
Phase 1 includes the customer application, a role-based Vendor Partner application containing Vendor Owner/Staff and 
Delivery Partner modes, and the Admin Operations Dashboard. Continuous live rider tracking is explicitly excluded.
Service-only commercial model
LNDRY is a laundry-service booking and fulfilment platform. Shirts, trousers, sarees, blankets, shoes and weight-based 
clothes are customer-owned articles handed over for cleaning or care. They function as garment-rate lines used to calculate 
the service charge by piece, pair or kilogram.
Service model interpretation
 The customer books a vendor-defined laundry service and selects the garment types, quantities or estimated weight to 
be processed.
 The amount displayed before pickup is an estimated service charge based on published vendor rates.
 After pickup, the vendor may confirm the actual garment count or measured weight. Any permitted final adjustment 
uses the same published service rates and is fully audited.
 Pickup and delivery refer to collection of the customer's garments and return of those garments after the selected 
laundry service is completed.
 The Phase 1 architecture must remain centred on service categories, vendor services, garment rates, pickup-slot 
capacity, service quotations, laundry processing and return delivery.
1. Service-platform objective
LNDRY is a multi-vendor laundry marketplace where customers discover eligible nearby laundry vendors, compare 
services and prices, select a vendor, schedule a 60-minute pickup slot, pay through Razorpay, and track status from 
vendor confirmation through pickup, processing and OTP-verified delivery.

3. Functional requirements by module
3.1 Authentication and account security
 Customer, vendor and rider login through mobile OTP.
 OTP challenge expiry, retry limit, resend cooldown and rate limiting.
 Role-scoped sessions when one phone belongs to more than one role.
 Admin dashboard login uses email/password plus mandatory second factor.
 Second factor supports authenticator TOTP; direct email/SMS OTP may be configured as fallback.
 Recovery codes, session revocation, forced password change and audit logs for admin security.
3.2 Vendor application and approval
 Owner details, business details, operating hours, working days, pickup/delivery availability and optional GST.
 Owner identity, shop photograph, registration document, GST certificate and configurable document types.
 Exact business pin with latitude and longitude.
 Requested radius is not final; admin approves the effective radius.
 Statuses: DRAFT, WAITING_FOR_APPROVAL, CORRECTION_REQUIRED, APPROVED, REJECTED, 
SUSPENDED.
 Approval, rejection and correction notifications by enabled channels.
3.3 Vendor public profile and service catalogue
 Public laundry name, logo/shop image, description, contact, hours, working days and pickup/delivery capability.
 Admin-managed categories such as Wash & Fold, Wash & Iron, Dry Cleaning, Ironing, Shoe Care, Blanket, Curtain 
and Carpet Cleaning.
 Vendor-defined service name, description, inclusions, exclusions, completion time and optional image.
 Clothing/garment types priced per piece, kilogram or pair with minimum and maximum quantity.
 A profile becomes discoverable only after vendor approval, activation, effective radius and at least one published valid 
service.
3.4 Capacity and pickup slots
 Vendor defines daily order limit, active days, 60-minute slots and maximum orders per slot.
 A vendor without configured capacity is unavailable.
 Only slots with remaining capacity are shown to customers.
 Booking confirmation creates a short-lived slot hold; payment failure, timeout or rejection releases capacity.
 Database-level atomic enforcement prevents two customers booking the final slot.
 Admin may inspect and apply audited temporary overrides.
3.5 Customer discovery and comparison
 Customer chooses current location, searches address or selects a saved address, then confirms an exact pin.
 Eligibility checks customer coordinates, vendor coordinates, approved radius, vendor status, service status, capacity 
and slot availability.
 Nearest eligible vendors appear first by default; the customer makes the final selection.
 Sorting: nearest, price low-to-high, price high-to-low, best rating and value for money.
 Filtering: available now and service/garment type.
 Search supports service name, category name, garment type and vendor name.
3.6 Quotation, order and payment
 Customer selects a laundry service, garment quantities or estimated weight, slot and address.
 Server calculates estimate using a service-rate snapshot; the mobile client cannot be source of truth for amount.
 Razorpay supports enabled UPI, card, net banking and wallet methods.
 After verified payment, order is sent only to the customer-selected vendor.
 Initial business status is Waiting for Vendor Confirmation.
 Vendor accepts or rejects; no response triggers configurable auto-reject.
 The system does not automatically assign another vendor after rejection.

3.7 Pickup, processing and delivery
 Accepted order receives an available pickup employee assignment; vendor may reassign.
 Rider opens exact customer location in external Google Maps and marks Going for Pickup.
 Pickup requires purpose-bound pickup OTP before completion.
 Vendor confirms Received at Partner and may confirm actual garment count or measured weight.
 Processing stages support Received, Washing, Drying, Ironing and Packed; irrelevant stages may be skipped through 
permitted transitions.
 Packed order receives delivery assignment; rider marks Out for Delivery.
 Delivery requires separate purpose-bound delivery OTP before Delivered.
3.8 Tracking, history, ratings and reports
 Customer sees status timeline and notifications, not a continuously moving rider marker.
 Order history includes vendor, garment lines, quantities/weight, timestamps, payment, final amount and status history.
 Only delivered orders are review-eligible.
 Vendor performance includes acceptance, completion, average delivery time, rating and counts.
 Vendor revenue includes daily, weekly, monthly and per-order details.
 Admin reports include order volume, GMV, customer activity, vendor performance, completion and cancellation.

6. Watermark and secure-document requirements
 Admin Dashboard includes a configurable watermark module with logo or text, position, scale, opacity and application 
profile.
 Marketing watermark may be applied to vendor service/shop images as a generated derivative while retaining the 
original according to policy.
 Sensitive KYC previews must display a dynamic visible watermark such as "For LNDRY Verification Only", 
vendor/application reference and view date.
 Original KYC files remain private; access uses short-lived signed URLs or authenticated streaming with audit logging.
 Watermark processing is server-side using Sharp/Cloudinary transformations and BullMQ for batch processing.
 Admin can preview, enable/disable, apply, retry failed jobs and inspect watermark history.
 Watermark is not a substitute for encryption, access control or document retention policy.

8. Data model requirements
 Core entities: users, roles, devices, addresses, vendors, vendor applications, documents, employees, categories, 
services, garment types, prices, recurring slots, slot exceptions, slot holds, orders, order garment lines, assignments, 
OTP challenges, payments, refunds, reviews, notifications, audit logs, watermark settings and asset jobs.
 Orders store immutable snapshots of vendor identity, address, service, service rate, selected garment 
quantity/estimated weight, slot and estimated amount.
 Price changes after booking do not retroactively change existing order snapshots.
 Final quantity/weight adjustments retain original and confirmed values plus reason and actor.
 Soft deletion is used for records that must remain referenced by historical orders.
 Every critical state change writes an order event with actor, old state, new state, timestamp and request ID.

10. Explicit Phase 1 exclusions
 Continuous live rider movement on the Customer App.
 Automatic reassignment to another vendor after rejection or timeout.
 Dynamic surge pricing, subscription plans, loyalty programmes or unrelated marketplace modules unless separately 
approved.
 In-app turn-by-turn navigation; rider uses external Google Maps.
 Automated government KYC verification unless a separate provider and commercial scope are approved.
 App Store/Play Store account fees, Google Maps charges, SMS charges, Razorpay charges, Cloudinary charges and 
cloud-hosting fees unless explicitly included in the commercial agreement.
11. Client-provided dependencies
 Approved brand assets, legal business name, privacy policy, terms, refund/cancellation policy and support contacts.
 Razorpay account and production keys with webhook access.
 Google Cloud project, Maps billing, API keys and package/domain restrictions.
 Firebase project access and platform configuration files.
 Cloudinary account or approved alternative.
 SMS/DLT account, approved templates and sender/entity details for India.
 Production domain, server/VPS or cloud account and DNS access.
 Final category, service, pricing, cancellation, auto-reject and refund business decisions.
12. Acceptance criteria
Area Acceptance condition
Vendor onboarding Vendor submits full application; admin can approve, reject or request 
correction; effective radius is admin-controlled.
Discovery Only eligible vendors within approved radius and with active 
service/capacity appear.
Capacity Concurrent attempts cannot exceed slot or daily capacity.
Payment Successful Razorpay signature creates one paid order; duplicate 
webhook does not duplicate order.
Vendor confirmation Vendor can accept/reject; configured timeout auto-rejects and notifies 
customer.
Pickup Only assigned rider can verify pickup OTP and complete pickup.
Processing Vendor can use permitted laundry states; customer sees grouped

Delivery Only assigned rider can verify delivery OTP and mark delivered.

delivered/rejected/cancelled orders.
13. Change-control rule
Any new feature not stated in this annexure is a change request. The change must record scope, API/database impact, 
user-interface impact, third-party cost, delivery impact and commercial approval before development begins.
